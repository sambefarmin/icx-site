Methodology
How ICX Calculates the Rate
Transparent. Repeatable. Independent of hardware cost models.
01

Collect

Gather executable on-demand hourly pricing from a diversified panel of US-based cloud providers. Multi-GPU clusters normalized to per-GPU equivalent. Automated scrapers run hourly; remaining providers updated manually.

02

Normalize

Standard Compute Unit: 1× NVIDIA H100 SXM5 80GB (NVLink), US-located, on-demand, hourly provisionable. PCIe H100 variants are tracked separately and excluded from the primary SXM5 index. All prices in $/GPU-hour.

03

Clean

Remove unavailable providers. Require a minimum panel of 8 available SXM5 providers — rate is not published if the panel is smaller. Trim the bottom and top 15% of remaining prices to remove statistical outliers.

04

Publish

Rate updated hourly from live scrapes. Each computation uses only currently-available providers, so the rate reflects executable spot prices — not stale listings.

05

Price ≠ Cost

ICX reflects transaction prices, not hardware ASP or production expense. Commodity benchmarks measure markets, not drilling costs.

06

Governance

Published methodology. Documented update process. Minimum data requirements enforced in code. Variant separation documented and applied consistently. The remedy for data risk is governance, not complexity.

Strategic Sequence
Benchmark First. Then Everything Else.
Derivatives cannot precede credibility. ICX follows the established path: measure → adopt → structure → financialize.
Phase 1 — Now

Measurement

Publish a consistent daily reference rate. Establish volatility patterns, seasonal shifts, and supply tightness in a neutral instrument.

Phase 2

Adoption

Startups benchmark vendor quotes to ICX. Procurement teams embed ICX in forecasts. The signal: "ICX plus spread" enters the market vocabulary.

Phase 3

Structure

Term expectations, forward curves, and realized volatility measures derived from observed spot data. Analytic layers, not separate businesses.

Phase 4

Financialization

Bilateral swaps. Cash-settled futures. Only after the benchmark is sufficiently trusted and liquid. Trust is the prerequisite, not the product.
